Reflection on "As We May Think"

By Ziqian (Catherine) Zhang

Technology has made many achievements that have made human life more satisfying. Productivity has increased dramatically, and communication across space and time has become easier. Scientists never reach the end of the field they need to explore.

In this reading, the author pointed out that human experience and research have been published and documented far more than we can actually put to use and expand in depth, with the result that some important milestones have been buried in insignificant records. But the author argued that this problem can be changed by sophisticated science. Records come in many different forms, and human beings are constantly developing techniques for recording information and transmitting it. The author provided a very professional description of this historical development.

In fact, the current technology, including AI, databases, etc., which can be considered whimsical technology at the time of the author, has now become mainstream, and there is a certain number of people with related knowledge keeping research and exploring. Search engines may not be fully perfected, but access to recent records is already in the hands of everyone, as long as one has a device that can connect to the Internet.
